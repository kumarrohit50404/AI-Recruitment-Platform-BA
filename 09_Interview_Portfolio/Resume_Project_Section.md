## 2. Resume Bullet Points (Select 4–6)

### Discovery & Requirements
- Documented business and system requirements in a structured BRD, translating recruiter and HR stakeholder pain points into prioritized needs with MoSCoW classification

- Supported stakeholder discovery across eight role groups (Recruiter, HR Manager, Compliance, Candidate, and others) through interviews and workshop notes; captured findings in a stakeholder analysis matrix

### Agile Specification & Traceability
- Wrote user stories and Gherkin-style acceptance criteria for core hiring workflows including AI resume screening, interview scheduling, and reporting

- Maintained a requirements traceability matrix linking business requirements to user stories and UAT scenarios to support validation planning

### Process & Solution Design
- Mapped AS-IS and TO-BE hiring processes to identify bottlenecks in manual screening, interview scheduling, and evaluation; proposed workflow improvements aligned to business rules

- Created wireframe specifications and a Figma implementation plan to visualize recruiter and HR manager workflows from documented requirements

### Data & Reporting (BA scope)
- Defined reporting requirements and KPIs (time-to-hire, pipeline status, offer acceptance) with formulas, data sources, and dashboard needs per stakeholder persona

- Developed sample SQL queries and an API integration summary to validate reporting requirements and document how system modules exchange data

### Validation
- Supported UAT planning by defining business acceptance scenarios in plain language and tracing them to requirements through the RTM

## 5. Interview Talking Points

### Opening statement (30 seconds)
> "This is a portfolio case study for HireFlow AI, a recruitment platform I documented as a Business Analyst exercise. I started with stakeholder interviews and pain points, wrote requirements in a BRD, modeled the hiring process, created user stories with acceptance criteria, and traced everything through an RTM to UAT scenarios. I can walk you through any of those artifacts."

---

### Talking point 1: Requirements approach
**Question:** "How did you gather and manage requirements?"

- Identified eight stakeholder groups and documented pain points in a stakeholder matrix
- Used interviews and workshop-style notes for elicitation (portfolio simulation)
- Documented requirements in a BRD with business rules and reporting needs
- Broke requirements into user stories with acceptance criteria for agile teams
- Linked requirements to validation through an RTM
- **Proof:** "I can trace a requirement like explainable AI screening from a compliance pain point to a user story and a UAT scenario."

---

### Talking point 2: Process improvement
**Question:** "Tell me about a process you improved."

- Mapped AS-IS hiring: manual screening, email-based scheduling, inconsistent evaluation
- Identified bottlenecks with recruiters and HR stakeholders
- Designed TO-BE flow with AI-assisted screening, structured scorecards, and dashboard reporting
- Documented in process flow diagrams
- **Proof:** "The TO-BE process shows where automation helps and where human review stays — for example, recruiters can override AI scores with documented justification."

---

### Talking point 3: Reporting & data (BA level)
**Question:** "How do you handle reporting requirements?"

- Started with stakeholder questions: what does HR need to see weekly vs. the recruiter daily?
- Documented reporting requirements and KPI definitions with formulas and owners
- Created a dashboard overview by persona (Executive, Recruiter, HR)
- Used sample SQL queries to sanity-check that KPI data could be retrieved
- Summarized API integration needs — not building APIs, but documenting what data must flow where
- **Proof:** "Time-to-hire KPI links to a reporting requirement, a dashboard widget, and a sample SQL validation query."

---

### Talking point 4: Stakeholder management
**Question:** "How do you handle conflicting stakeholder needs?"

- Example: recruiters wanted speed; compliance wanted audit trails on AI decisions
- Resolution documented as requirements and business rules — configurable thresholds plus mandatory audit logging
- Used a power-interest grid to plan who to engage closely vs. keep informed
- **Proof:** "Conflicts became explicit requirements instead of staying as hallway debates."

---

### Talking point 5: UAT & validation
**Question:** "How do you know requirements are testable?"

- Wrote acceptance criteria in Given/When/Then format for priority stories
- RTM maps requirements → stories → UAT scenarios
- UAT scenarios written in business language for recruiter and HR reviewers
- **Proof:** "UAT-002 traces to the screening efficiency requirement — a recruiter can walk through it without reading technical test scripts."
